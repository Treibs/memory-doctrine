# Tulving & Thomson 1973 — Encoding specificity

The foundational demonstration that retrieval cue effectiveness is determined by the
degree to which the cue reinstates the *specific encoding context*, not by its general
semantic relatedness to the target. Participants recalled target words (e.g., COLD)
better with a weak cue present at encoding (e.g., ground) than with a strong
semantic associate (e.g., hot) that was absent during encoding — even though hot is a
much stronger associate of COLD outside of context. The principle: the trace
specifies which cues will work; the system does not retrieve on "closest semantic
neighbor" but on "best cue–trace overlap."
