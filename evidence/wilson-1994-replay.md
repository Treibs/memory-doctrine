# Wilson & McNaughton 1994 — Reactivation of hippocampal ensemble memories during sleep

The founding experimental evidence that offline hippocampal replay is a real
phenomenon and a candidate engine of systems consolidation. [[D3-consolidation-mtt-safe]]
uses this to ground the claim that consolidation is a **scheduled offline batch
process** (a replay-pass cron), distinct from real-time ingestion.

The experience-specificity of the replay (correlation structure matches exploration
session, not pre-exploration baseline) establishes that it carries content, not just
noise. This licenses the KPM analogue: a replay cron that re-walks recent high-value
axioms and strengthens the connections between them, then promotes the durable ones
to the stable doctrine tier.
