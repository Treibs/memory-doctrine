# Quine 1951 — Two Dogmas of Empiricism

W.V.O. Quine's landmark paper rejected the analytic/synthetic distinction and
verification individualism, arguing that "our statements about the external world face
the tribunal of sense experience not individually but only as a corporate body." This
generates the *web of belief* picture: a network of mutually supporting beliefs where
no single claim is immune to revision in principle, but the core (logic, mathematics)
is protected by its massive entanglement with everything else — pragmatic entrenchment,
not foundational immunity.

Used by [[F2-contradictions-category-errors]] to support the split-rule: the
foundationalism/coherentism dichotomy partially dissolves under Quine's picture (the
"core" of the web behaves foundationally; the "periphery" behaves coherentistically —
the apparent contradiction was a false dichotomy). Also grounds [[A3-foundherentist-generativity]]'s
"generativity = entrenchment" thesis: high-generativity axioms sit at the web's center
and are revised last, matching AGM's epistemic-entrenchment ordering.
