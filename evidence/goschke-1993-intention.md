# Goschke & Kuhl 1993 — Intention-Superiority Effect

Demonstrates the **intention-superiority effect**: items from a to-be-*executed* script are
recognized significantly faster than items from a to-be-*memorized* script, establishing that
open intentions are held at **heightened activation** in memory — they are pre-activated,
making them spontaneously accessible.

Crucially, the effect **reverses on completion**: once the goal is discharged, related items
become *slower* than neutral items, indicating an **active inhibition / de-activation** that
suppresses the now-irrelevant intention and prevents perseveration.

This gives a principled lifecycle: `pending → heightened-activation → fired → inhibited`.
The boost is strictly an *activation / retrievability* signal, not an evidential confidence
claim — consistent with [[D1-retrievability-decay]] and the [[C2-three-orderings]] firewall.
