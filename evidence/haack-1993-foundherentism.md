# Haack 1993 — Evidence and Inquiry (Foundherentism)

Susan Haack's *Evidence and Inquiry* (1993) coins **foundherentism** as the hybrid
epistemic position that the KPM doctrine formally instantiates. The central
argument: both pure foundationalism (which needs an immune, unjustifiable base)
and pure coherentism (which cannot escape the isolation objection — a coherent
fairy tale could be internally consistent yet false) fail on their own. The correct
structure is the **crossword analogy**:

- Horizontal connections (across entries) = coherentist mutual support: entries
  constrain each other, and the system is stronger for it.
- Vertical grounding (entries anchored to the grid/clues) = foundational
  non-circular grounding: some entries are directly checked against external
  evidence.
- Neither dimension is sufficient alone; both are necessary.

The *warrant calculus* Haack proposes — justification is a function of
**supportiveness** × **independent security** × **comprehensiveness** — maps
directly onto the KPM's `confidence` scoring logic.

Used by [[A3-foundherentist-generativity]]: the KPM's axiom graph is a
foundherentist object. High-generativity axioms are the crossword "anchors" —
revised last, maximally entrenched — while derived findings form the coherent
mutual-support web around them.
