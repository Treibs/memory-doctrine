# Nadel & Moscovitch 1997 — Multiple-Trace Theory (MTT)

The canonical source for the Multiple-Trace Theory challenge to the standard
systems-consolidation model. [[D3-consolidation-mtt-safe]] cites this as the primary
reason the promote-and-detach policy is unsafe until the debate is resolved.

MTT's core claim: each retrieval of a rich episodic memory forms a new hippocampal
trace (multiple traces), so the hippocampus is never truly dispensable for episodic
detail. The gist/semantic version may transfer to cortex, but the contextually rich
original stays hippocampus-dependent.

The KPM safety implication: if MTT is correct, an axiom promoted-and-detached from
the index becomes unretrievable for its episodic provenance (context, source, when
it was learned). Even if the gist survives in the doctrine tier, the source-binding
that makes it *verified* (C1) may require the index. Promote-and-keep-indexed is the
MTT-safe policy that hedges both outcomes.
