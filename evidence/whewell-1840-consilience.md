# Whewell 1840 — The Philosophy of the Inductive Sciences

William Whewell coined *consilience of inductions* to describe the epistemic upgrade that
occurs when a theory derived from one class of phenomena turns out to explain, without
modification, a wholly different class. He used Newton's gravitation as the canonical case:
derived from planetary orbits, it also explained tides, the precession of equinoxes, and
the shape of the Earth — facts from entirely separate observation classes. The fact that
the law "jumps across" without ad hoc modification is the strongest non-deductive warrant
an empirical theory can receive.

Used by [[F1-convergence-corroboration]] as the foundational formulation of the
corroboration-by-independent-convergence heuristic. The doctrine operationalizes it as:
convergence scores when the converging traditions are genuinely independent in method,
motivation, and data — borrowed re-labellings do not multiply evidence.
