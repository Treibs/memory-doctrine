# Roediger & Karpicke 2006 — Test-Enhanced Learning

The landmark two-experiment paper establishing the **testing effect** (retrieval-practice
effect) in controlled laboratory conditions. Participants studied prose passages in one
of three formats: study-only (SSSS), one test (STTT), or three tests (STTT with
varying ratios). At a 5-minute delay, study-only participants outperformed on free
recall. At a one-week delay, the pattern reversed dramatically: the test group retained
significantly more than the study-only group.

The paper demonstrates that retrieval practice is not merely a measurement tool but
an encoding event — the act of retrieving strengthens the trace in a way that
additional study does not. The delay-dependence is the critical honesty qualifier:
retrieval practice *appears* to hurt in the short run (more errors during practice)
but produces superior long-term durability.

Used by [[E2-retrieval-practice]] as the primary empirical ground for building
spaced re-validation into the KPM lifecycle.
