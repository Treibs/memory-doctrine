# Anderson, Bjork & Bjork 1994 — Retrieval-Induced Forgetting (RIF)

The canonical demonstration that retrieval practice on some items *actively
suppresses* retrievability of related unpracticed items. [[D1-retrievability-decay]]
treats this as Driver 2 (competition) — distinct from Driver 1 (passive disuse
decay) and Driver 3 (deliberate directed forgetting). The cue-independence test
(items tested with novel cues not present during practice) rules out a pure
fan-effect explanation (A1's budget), implicating an executive inhibitory mechanism.

KPM implication: near-duplicate axioms that compete for the same retrieval cue will
suppress each other's retrievability. Dedup and merge is not cosmetic — it removes
a structural source of mutual retrieval degradation.
