# Constantinescu, O'Reilly & Behrens 2016 — Gridlike Code for Conceptual Knowledge

The landmark fMRI study that established the grid-code for non-spatial, abstract
knowledge. Participants learned to navigate a 2-D "bird space" defined by neck length
and leg length. During conceptual navigation, entorhinal cortex and vmPFC produced the
hexagonal 6-fold rotational symmetry signal characteristic of grid cells during physical
navigation. The same neural substrate — not just an analogous one.

Used by [[F4-cognitive-map-unification]] as the primary evidence that B1's
"embeddings-as-geometry" is not a borrowed metaphor: the brain uses the same grid-code
to organize conceptual knowledge as it uses to organize physical space. This is the
strongest single piece of evidence for the B1 ≡ B4 ≡ cognitive-map unification. The
limits field carries the essential honesty clause: the result applies to 2-D continuous
concept spaces, and extrapolation to high-D discrete KG embeddings is not yet established.
