# McDaniel & Einstein 2000 — Multiprocess Framework for Prospective Memory

Establishes prospective memory as "the ability to remember to execute delayed intentions" —
distinct from retrospective recall in requiring **self-initiated retrieval at an unprompted
future time or event**. The multiprocess framework specifies two pathways:

- **Monitoring (strategic):** top-down maintenance + active scanning; imposes measurable
  ongoing-task cost; unsustainable over long delays.
- **Spontaneous retrieval:** reflexive cue-driven "pop into mind"; no sustained cost.

**Focality** governs the split: cues *already processed* by the ongoing task (focal) support
cheap spontaneous retrieval; nonfocal cues require costly monitoring.

Extended by Einstein et al. (2005, *J. Exp. Psychol. General*, 134, 327–342;
PubMed 16131267) in a direct multiprocess test, and by Scullin, McDaniel & Shelton (2013,
*Cognitive Psychology*, PMC3809757) showing *context-gated* monitoring — agents monitor
selectively near the trigger window, not continuously.
