# Bjork 1989 — Directed Forgetting as Retrieval Inhibition

Establishes intentional/directed forgetting as a third, distinct driver of
retrievability loss alongside passive disuse decay and retrieval-induced forgetting
competition. [[D1-retrievability-decay]] lists this as Driver 3 (deliberate intent).

The key distinction from RIF ([[anderson-1994-rif]]): directed forgetting is
*instructed* and *intentional*, not a by-product of strengthening competing items.
It is the biological precursor to the KPM's [[D5-suppress]] operator — the first
genuinely agentic memory verb (per the spine's v1.1 addition). Bjork's finding that
these effects are partially reversible supports D5's reversibility clause: a
suppressed axiom must remain recoverable under adversarial / E4 audit.
