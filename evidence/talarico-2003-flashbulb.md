# Talarico & Rubin 2003 — Confidence, Not Consistency, Characterizes Flashbulb Memories

The clean demonstration that salience inflates confidence and vividness without improving accuracy: flashbulb memories feel more accurate and stay confident across time, yet they are no more factually consistent than ordinary memories. Refutes Brown & Kulik's (1977) "Now Print!" indelibility hypothesis. Used by [[C4-salience-gating]] to establish that salience is a promotion signal, not a confidence signal — these must be stored as two orthogonal scores.
