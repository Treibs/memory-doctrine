# Laird, Lebiere & Rosenbloom 2017 — A Standard Model of the Mind

Documents the convergence of three competing cognitive architectures (SOAR, ACT-R, Sigma)
on a shared structural skeleton — the Common Model of Cognition — despite being developed
by rival teams from different foundational commitments. Canonical structural commitments
shared across all three: declarative vs. procedural memory as a non-collapsible distinction;
modular structure with buffers; a central serial production cycle as bottleneck; episodic
and semantic stores as distinct long-term memory types.

Used by [[F1-convergence-corroboration]] as a strong empirical corroboration instance:
40 years of competition produced convergence, not divergence — the structural commitments
that survived multiple independent challenges are the load-bearing ones. The limits field
notes partial ancestry overlap (Newell's influence) reducing the independence credit vs.
the Schultz 1997 physics-meets-algorithm case.
