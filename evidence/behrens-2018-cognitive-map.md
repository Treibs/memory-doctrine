# Behrens et al. 2018 — What Is a Cognitive Map?

The most comprehensive unifying review of the cognitive-map literature, synthesizing
place cells, grid cells, entorhinal cortex, and conceptual-space research into a single
theoretical framework: a cognitive map is a *factorized* representation of relational
structure. The factorization — structure separate from content — is why maps generalize.
Grid cells are read as the neural instantiation of this structural generalization
mechanism: they encode abstract geometric structure (the "ruler") independent of the
specific sensory content at each location.

Used by [[F4-cognitive-map-unification]] as the primary theoretical justification for
the claim that "a KPM is structurally a factorized cognitive map." The factorization
thesis directly maps onto the doctrine's architecture: generators/axioms = the relational
structure (the portable part); evidence in `research/` = the content (the location-specific
part filled in on demand). Also supports [[B4-index-store-split]] (the factorized map
*is* the sparse-index architecture) and [[E1-layered-distillation]] (distill the
structure, leave the content below).
