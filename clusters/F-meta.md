# F · Meta

Convergence across independent disciplines is a corroboration heuristic, not proof — except where the convergence is a proven identity (Hopfield = attention); treat structural correspondences as support, not certification. Most apparent contradictions in the doctrine turn out to be category errors: two distinct quantities the literature had already split, which a careful reading resolves by splitting, not choosing sides. The Surprise Principle unifies salience-gating, novelty-gated write, and predictive coding as one computational quantity — signed prediction error — at three levels of description, giving the doctrine its write policy: no-PE reinforces retrievability, moderate-PE reconsolidates, large-PE mints a new node. And the B1 = B4 unification reveals that the KPM is structurally a factorized cognitive map — the sparse generator layer (structure) factored from the rich content layer (evidence) — which is exactly why generative axioms transfer across domains.
