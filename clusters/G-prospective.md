# G · Prospective

Prospective memory — remembering to act at a future moment — is a categorically distinct system from the retrospective recall that clusters A–F describe, and agentic KPMs require it explicitly. Focal/event-triggered intentions bind a cue to a KG node the agent already traverses, making retrieval free; monitoring/polling is costed and should be gated to the deadline window. An open intention sits at heightened activation (intention-superiority effect) and must be actively inhibited on completion — failure to close an intention causes the agent to persevere on finished goals, the agentic analogue of the human Zeigarnik persistence failure. This cluster is scoped to agentic KPMs; a pure reference KPM may omit G.
