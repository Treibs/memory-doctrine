# C · Truth

Confidence is a credence — a graded degree of belief earned exclusively by verified external evidence and revised by conditionalization; it must never be inferred from retrieval fluency, recency, salience, or embedding similarity, all of which are systematically miscalibrated proxies. The graph carries three strictly independent orderings — evidential confidence, entrenchment (generativity), and retrievability — and conflating any two is a category error with real failure consequences. Even high confidence is no guarantee of correctness: spreading-activation stores confidently confabulate plausible interpolations that were never stored, and arousal buys consolidation and felt-vividness but not accuracy.
